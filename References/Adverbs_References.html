ADVERBS

Reference/Adverbs

By looking at this Reference you can Understand the concept of What is Adverb, what are the types of Adverbs. also It will guide you to answer the tasks provided.

You can Refer this References for Further Knowledge or Knowledge gaining Purposes

In this lesson we are going to learn about Adverbs.
Examples :-
1. He walked slowly across the square.
2.Tom is very tall.

In the above example that the Adverb ‘slowly’ is describing the Verb ‘walk’ by telling that the person was walking slowly.

DEFINITION :-

Adverbs are words that are used in sentences to describe or change the meaning of a Verb or Adjective or even another Adverb. They add a description to the sentence to make it more detailed and interesting.


Types of Adverbs

* Adverbs are used in sentences to answer many questions about the Verbs/Adjectives/Adverbs themselves. The different types of Adverbs are as following:


* Now let’s refer each category of ADVERBS in deep with some example sentences.


1. Adverb of Time

Example :- The results were announced yesterday

Here the Adverb is yesterday which is answering the question: When were the results announced? ‘Announced’ is the verb in this sentence.
Other examples of Adverbs of Time are – Once, Never, Tomorrow, Daily, etc.


2. Adverb of Place

Example :- They will meet you there.

The Adverb here is there that is specifying a place for the Verb meet and the question being answered is: Where will they meet you?
Other examples of Adverbs of Place are – Anywhere, Somewhere, Near, Far,etc.


3. Adverb of Manner

Example :- He quietly slipped away.

The Adverb here is quietly which is telling the way or manner in which the action was carried out and the Verb is slipped which is telling: How did he slip away. The Verb here is work and the Adverb is fast and the question being asked is: How does she work?
Other examples of Adverbs of Manner are – slowly,brightly,louly,etc.


4. Adverb of Frequency

Example :- He likes to watch TV every day.

Here, the Adverb is every day and it is telling about the amount of time spent in doing the Verb, which is watch. The question in this sentence is: How often does he watch TV?


These Adverbs are used to show the duration or timing of the action that is happening/had happened/will happen. They also tell us how often and how long these actions would be. Other examples of Adverbs of Frequency are – Frequently, Often, Yearly, Briefly, etc.


5. Adverbs of Degree

Example :- She almost finished the work.

The Verb here is finished and the Adverb is almost which is telling us about the amount of the work finished. The question being asked is: How much of the work did she finish?


The Adverbs of Degree are used to show to what extent or how much has an action been done or will be done. Other examples of these Adverbs are – Fully, Partially, Altogether, etc.


6. Adverbs of Confirmation and Negation

Example :- He likes to watch TV every day.

The Adverb here is certainly which is reinforcing the Verb like in answer to the question: Will they like this vase?


These Adverbs either confirm or deny the action of the Verb. They are also used to reinforce the action that is described by the Verb. Other examples of Adverbs of Confirmation are – Definitely, Absolutely, Surely, etc.
