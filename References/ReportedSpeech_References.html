GRAMMER

Referance/Reported Speech

What is Reported Speech?

Reported speech which is also called indirect speech is a way of conveying what a person has said without quoting the exact words used by the speaker that mean If we want to say what other people said, thought or felt, we can use the direct and indirect speech (reported speech). It converts the direct speech to indirect one.

For example,

If Karthik said "I am not going on the trip." You will convey this to Suthahar by saying Karthik said that he was not going on the trip. Note the grammatical differences betw een the original utterance and the reported utterance.

One more example,

Nimal said, "I like cakes"
Nimal said that he liked cakes.

The process

The following changes are made,

1. Inverted com mas are removed.
2. The clause begins with "that".
3. Tense is changed.
4. Pronoun is changed.

Tense changes

1. Present ------------> Past

is/am was
are were
drink drank

Manel told me "I never understand you,"
Manel told me that she never understood me.

2. Present Continuous ------------> Past Continuous

is/am working was working
are working were working

Ravi said "We are doing exercises,"
Ravi said that they were doing exercises.

3. Present Perfect------------> Past Perfect

has worked had worked
have worked had worked

The child said, "I have broken the window,"
The child said that he had broken the window.

4. Past ------------> Past Perfect

was had been
were had been
went had gone

I said, "She went to Rom e,"
I said that she had gone to Rome.

5. Present Continuous ------------> Past Perfect Continuous

was planning had been planning
were planning had been planning

Mary said. "He was thinking of buying a new car"
Mary said that he had been thinking of buying a new car.

Future forms

1. Will ------------> Would

Pasindu reminded me "I will come on Sunday,"
Pasindu reminded me that he would come on Sunday.

2. Shall ------------> Would

He said, "I shall appreciate it"
He said he would appreciate it.

3. May ------------> Might

She promised, "I may write to him"
She promised that she might write to him.

4. Can ------------> Could

Ravi said, "I can speak English"
Ravi said that he could speak English.

The verb forms remain the same in the following cases.

> If the reporting verb is in the present tense.

Uchana says, "I will never go to Work"
Uchana says she will never go to work.

> When we report something that is still true.

The teacher said, "Asia is the largest continent."
The teacher said that Asia is the largest continent.

Some examples

Ruwan: "Our teacher will go to Kandy tomorrow."
Ruwan said that their teacher would go to Kandy the next day.

Manel: "I was washing clothes yesterday."
Manel told that she had been washing clothes the day before.

Questions

> In questions we do the tense and pronoun changes as we did earlier
> But we don't use that clause.
> We change the word order from questions to affirmative.

Examples:

The teacher asked me, "Where do you live?"
The teacher asked me where I lived.

Mother asked Nimal, "What were you doing?"
Mother asked Nimal what he had been doing.

> When there is no question word (question requires 'yes' or 'No' answers) will take 'if' or 'whether' in the reported speech.

My friend asked me “Do like chocolate?”
My friend asked me whether I liked chocolate.

Chamil asked “Can I see the video?”
Chamil asked whether he could see the video.

Commands / Requests

> In requests or commands we use a word of command like "told, ordered, asked, requested, and warned"
> After the name we use 'to' and the verb in infinitive.

Examples:

Teacher told m e "Close the door."
Teacher told m e to close the door.

"Please help me" the land lady requested.
The landlady requested me to help her.

Negatives

> In negatives we use ''not to''.

Example:

"Please don't touch the flow ers," the gardener said.
The gardener requested us not to touch the flowers.
