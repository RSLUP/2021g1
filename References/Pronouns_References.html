In this lesson we are going to learn about PRONOUNS. Usually we know nouns, nouns are words that refers to specify thing or person.
Example:- Umbrella, Phones, Nick, Tree, etc.
But PRONOUNS in other hands, takes the place of a noun. Those are major subclass of nouns.
Example :- I, we, you, they, etc.
Let’s focus on a proper Definition for PRONOUNS

DEFINITION :- PRONOUNS is a word that can function by itself as a noun phrase.

Now let’s refer each category of PRONOUNS in deep with some example sentences.

Possessive PRONOUNS
Possessive PRONOUNS show that something belongs to someone. The possessive PRONOUNS
are my, our, your, his, her, its, and their. There's also an “independent” form of each of these
PRONOUNS: mine, ours, yours, his, hers, its, and theirs.

Examples
This is my Book
This book is Ours
This is our School
This School is theirs.


Reflexive PRONOUNS
Reflexive PRONOUNS are words like myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves,
yourselves and themselves. They refer back to a person or thing. We often use reflexive
PRONOUNS when the subject and the object of a verb are the same.

Examples
I can complete the work myself.
They can drive the car Themselves.
He can pass the exam himself.


Reciprocal PRONOUNS
We use reciprocal PRONOUNS when each of two or more subjects is acting in the same way
towards the other. There must be two or more. They must be doing the same thing. Don’t use
the Reciprocal PRONOUNS as the subject. There is another name for Reciprocal PRONOUN is
Compound PRONOUN

Examples
A is talking to B, B is talking to A. So, we can say A and B are talking to Each other.
We all try and help One another.
They help One another.
Why don’t you believe Each other.


Relative PRONOUNS
A relative PRONOUN is a word that introduces a dependent (or relative) clause and connects it to an independent clause. A clause beginning with a relative PRONOUN is poised to answer questions such as Which one? How many? or What kind? Who, whom, what, which, and that.

Examples
The cat that stole the pie is back.
The person who bought his car is a businessman.
They young girl whose nails are sharp.


Demonstrative PRONOUNS
A demonstrative PRONOUN is a PRONOUN that represents a noun and expresses its position as near or far (including in time). The demonstrative PRONOUNS are "this," "that," "these," and "those."

Examples
This is my dad’s new Car.
These are my brother’s Books.
That is not a playground, but kids play.


Interrogative PRONOUNS
The main interrogative PRONOUNS are "what," "which”, “why”, "who," "whom," and "whose." Interrogative PRONOUNS are used to ask questions.

Examples
What did he say to you?
Who do you want to know?
Which one do you like?
Why are the scolding you?


Indefinite PRONOUNS
An indefinite PRONOUN is a pronoun that refers to a person or a thing without being specific. In short, Indefinite PRONOUNS does not refer to a specific person or thing.

Examples
There is something in my shoe.
Would you like another drink?
I need some stamps.
Anyone can do this work.
